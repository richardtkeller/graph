About Me

I'm Dr. Richard T. Mayer, a Senior Principal Engineer in Medical Safety at Johnson & Johnson MedTech and Interventional Oncology. With over two decades in engineering and 19 years dedicated to healthcare innovation, I have led multidisciplinary teams across continents, driving patient safety, benefit-risk quantification, and clinical applicability in R&D strategy.

My background spans biomechanics, orthopaedic research, and leadership in global MedTech initiatives, including quantifiable safety frameworks and cross-functional collaborations with regulatory authorities.

Beyond my professional career, I contribute as an Honorary Surgeon with the Wildlife Sanctuary Foundation and am an active member of the Orthopaedic Research Society (ORS) and the Swiss Society for Biomedical Engineering (SSBE).

About the Course

Generative AI for Work & Research explores how modern AI tools can augment critical thinking, automate workflows, and enhance creativity in scientific and industrial settings. The course focuses on practical applications of AI models like GPT, diffusion systems, and multimodal architectures for literature analysis, concept design, and data storytelling.

My goal in this course is to integrate generative AI responsibly into research and engineering methodologies — enabling safer, faster, and more insightful development of MedTech solutions while maintaining ethical and regulatory compliance.
